# Luna Trust Sections Pre-Launch Checklist

These trust sections intentionally use placeholders until Luna has real proof, real consent, and real coverage. Do not launch public marketing with unverified claims.

## Placeholder Content To Replace

- Medical advisors in `components/marketing/MedicalAdvisors.tsx`
  - Replace placeholder names, credentials, affiliations, quotes, and portraits.
  - Verify board certification, menopause-specialist credentials, and any society memberships.
  - Get signed permission to use each advisor's name, photo, credentials, and quote.
- Testimonials in `components/marketing/Testimonials.tsx`
  - Replace all quote copy, names, ages, journey context, and avatar assets.
  - Collect written consent before using first name, last initial, age, location/context, quote, or photo.
  - Keep the emotional arc true to the user's actual story.
- Press and credibility in `components/marketing/PressStrip.tsx`
  - Current strip avoids press logos because coverage has not been verified in this repo.
  - Add publication logos only after real coverage exists and logo usage is permitted.
- Trust metrics
  - Replace "Trusted by 5,000+ women" and "4.8 average rating from 1,200+ women" if those numbers are not yet true and documented.

## Asset Requirements

- `/public/images/advisors/advisor-1.jpg` and `advisor-2.jpg`
  - 400x400 professional headshots.
  - Real advisors only, with signed usage permission.
- `/public/images/testimonials/sarah.jpg`, `maya.jpg`, `priya.jpg`, and `diane.jpg`
  - 200x200 warm portraits from real users with signed consent.
  - Use neutral initials avatars until consented photos exist.
- `/public/images/press/producthunt.svg` and other press logos as earned.
  - Use logos only with proof of coverage and permitted brand usage.

## Legal And Ethical Guardrails

- Never fabricate testimonials, advisor credentials, review scores, press mentions, compliance badges, or medical claims.
- Never use stock photography or AI-generated faces for advisors or testimonials.
- Do not claim "clinically proven", "FDA approved", "HIPAA compliant", or "SOC 2 compliant" unless documentation exists.
- Keep Luna positioned as a wellness companion, not a medical provider.
- Preserve privacy language in plain human terms: what is collected, what is deleted, and what is never shared.
